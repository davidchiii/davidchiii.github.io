What is causing the decline of biodiversity in our oceans?

climate change

Rising sea temperatures due to climate change result in coral bleaching, where corals expel the symbiotic algae (zooxanthellae) that provide them with energy and color. Repeated or prolonged bleaching events can lead to coral death.

acidification

Increased carbon dioxide (CO2) levels in the atmosphere are absorbed by the oceans, causing the water to become more acidic. This makes it harder for corals to build their calcium carbonate skeletons and weakens their overall health.

Runoff from agriculture, urban areas, and industrial activities can introduce pollutants such as nutrients and chemicals into coastal waters, leading to water quality degradation and the proliferation of harmful algae that can smother corals.
